Beef Curry

Description

Beef curry is a rich, flavorful dish made with tender pieces of beef slow-cooked in a blend of aromatic spices. The meat absorbs the spices beautifully, creating a deep and comforting flavor that pairs perfectly with rice, naan, or roti.

This recipe is simple but full of traditional curry taste. It's ideal for family meals or special gatherings, and the leftovers taste even better the next day.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef (cut into medium pieces)
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2–3 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder or garam masala
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 cup water
  • Fresh coriander leaves (optional for garnish)

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pot and sauté the onions until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add tomatoes and cook until they soften and release oil.
  4. Add turmeric, cumin, coriander, and curry powder. Stir well.
  5. Add the beef pieces and mix until coated with the spices.
  6. Cook the beef for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add water, cover the pot, and let the beef simmer on low heat until tender.
  8. Once cooked, garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired.
  9. Serve hot with rice, naan, or roti.
